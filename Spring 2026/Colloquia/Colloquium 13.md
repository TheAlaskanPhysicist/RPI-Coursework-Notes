#### Abstract
Ultra-wide bandgap III-nitride semiconductors, such as AlN and GaN, offer unique opportunities for high-power, high-frequency, and extreme-environment electronics. In this talk, I will discuss recent progress in III-nitride electronic and acoustic devices, with a focus on their underlying device physics and performance limits. I will highlight how polarization effects and electro-acoustic coupling enable multifunctional behavior, allowing a single platform to support signal amplification, filtering, and sensing. Recent work in my group demonstrates monolithically integrated devices that combine these functions within a unified framework. These results point toward new pathways for multifunctional electronic systems operating across a wide range of conditions.

#### Biography
Wenwen Zhao is an Assistant Professor in the Department of Electrical, Computer, and Systems Engineering at Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute. She received her Ph.D. in Applied Physics from Cornell University in 2025. At RPI, her group explores ultra-wide-bandgap semiconductors, with an emphasis on the monolithic integration of acoustic and electronic devices for RF applications, multifunctional sensing, and quantum technologies, as well as the fundamental physical limits that govern device performance.
