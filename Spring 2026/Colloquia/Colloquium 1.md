#### Abstract
In this talk, I will overview my research in quantum information science and technology (QIST), spanning quantum communication and sensing. Drawing from my DOE CAREER Award, I discuss heterogeneous quantum repeaters integrating semiconductor quantum dots (QDs) with atomic ensembles for telecom-compatible, long-distance networks. Key pillars include transduction via four-wave mixing, optoelectronic device development with high-efficiency antennas, and advanced instrumentation like ultra-short pulse generators. I will compare QD and SPDC sources, highlighting deterministic emission and high emission rates. Additionally, as co-PI on quantum sensing projects, I present NV-center diamond devices for high-resolution strain and electric field detection, enabling directional particle detection in high-energy physics and medicine. Future directions include protocol benchmarking and on-chip integration.
