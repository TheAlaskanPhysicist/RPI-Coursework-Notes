**Title:** *Emergent Quantum Phenomena in Graphene: Condensed Matter Physics Through a Piece of Carbon*
**Speaker:** *Dr. Long Ju, Ph.D.*, Massachusetts Institute of Technology
**Understanding:** High

---
> Briefly describe in words the key concepts of the talk that interested you or if you did not find the talk interesting, why it was not interesting to you.

I found the flat banding inducing a break in time reversal symmetry to be interest, but especially the idea that there's a Fractional Quantum Hall Effect at $0$ field, being both Fractional and Anomalous at the same time. I didn't expect Moiré to contribute to such an effect.

---
> If you were the speaker what would you do differently?

I think I would have spent a bit more time describing the similarities and differences between FQAHE and FQHE, as well as FQAHE and QAHE, to showcase how these two adjustments lead to expected and unique behaviors.
