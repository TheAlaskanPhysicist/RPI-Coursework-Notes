> Briefly describe in words the key concepts of the talk that interested you or if you did not find the talk interesting, why it was not interesting to you.

I found the idea of using a cavity to effectively select a narrow band of light to be interesting, very similar to how radios operate with a narrow band for screening out other frequencies. Using the long half-life of inter-4F orbitals was pretty cool as well, since I recall transition frequencies being inverse proportional to the energy of transition.

I've heard of the Purcell effect, but I never actually understood what it was.

---
> If you were the speaker what would you do differently?

I think it was a little over my head in terms of photonics, mostly in some of the words they used. I think it would have been nice to have it be more general-physics friendly.
