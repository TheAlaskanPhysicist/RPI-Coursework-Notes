#### Abstract
In a quantum network, quantum information is transmitted between the quantum nodes around the world by optical photons. At the nodes, quantum information is generated, processed, stored and relayed by different types of qubits. Therefore, there should be a quantum interface at each node to generate photons and to convert quantum information from photons to other type of qubits, and vice versa. In this colloquium, I will present three solid-state platforms for photon generation, processing and interfacing. On the first platform, we doped rare-earth ions in a silicon nitride ring resonator to enhance the light-matter interaction. We achieved a ninefold Purcell enhancement at ultra-low temperature. This enhancement allowed us to perform photon-echo experiment in the device, which is a basic operation of a quantum memory for photons. On the second platform, we embedded a self-assembled indium arsenide quantum dot in a nano-beam waveguide. We demonstrated the single-photon nonlinearity, and the transmission through the waveguide is correlated with the spin state of the quantum dot. To prepare the spin state in a superposition, we further demonstrated coherent spin rotation and Ramsey interference. The third platform is 2D materials. We studied interlayer excitons in a 2D hetero-bilayer. In particular, we focused on the photon statistics of the light emitted from the excitons. We observed a transition from a thermal state to photon bunching and then possibly a coherent state. This behavior may indicate a phase transition from weakly interacting excitons to a Bose-Einstein condensate. At the end, I will briefly discuss my research proposal to study quantum optics and to discover new quantum emitters on the platform of 2D materials.
