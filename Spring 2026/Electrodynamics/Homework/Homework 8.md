## Question 12.9
> The magnetic field of the earth can be represented approximately by a magnetic dipole of magnetic moment $M=8.1\cdot10^{25}\text{ gauss-cm}^3$. Consider the motion of energetic electrons in the neighborhood of the earth under the action of this dipole field (Van Allen electron belts).



---
### Question 12.9.A
> Show that the equation for a line of magnetic force is $r=r_0\sin^2\theta$, where $\theta$ is the usual polar angle (colatitude) measured from the axis of the dipole, and find an expression for the magnitude of $B$ along any line of force as a function of $\theta$.



---
### Question 12.9.B
> A positively charged particle circles around a line of force in the equatorial plane with a gyration radius $a$ and a mean radius $R$ $(a\ll R)$. Show that the particle's azimuthal position (east longitude) changes approximately linearly in time according to
> $$\begin{align}
\phi(t)&=\phi_0-\dfrac{3}{2}\left(\dfrac{a}{R}\right)^2\omega_B(t-t_0)
\end{align}$$
> where $\omega_B$ is the frequency of gyration at radius $R$.



---
### Question 12.9.C
> If, in addition to its circular motion of [[#Question 12.9.B|part b]], the particle has a small component of velocity parallel to the lines of force, show that it undergoes small oscillations in $\theta$ around $\theta=\pi/2$ with a frequency $\Omega=\left(3/\sqrt{2}\right)(a_0/R)\omega_B$. Find the change in longitude per cycle of oscillation in latitude.

Calculate the same quantities for an electron of $10\text{ keV}$ as the same radius.
