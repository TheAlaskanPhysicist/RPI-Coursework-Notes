



## Structure of Vectors
For the sake of myself, I am going to use the following:
$$\begin{align}
\ket{x}&=x^ie_i & \bra{x}&=x_ie^i\\
\ket{e_i}&=e_i & \bra{e^i}&=e^i\\
\end{align}$$
We can define a metric tensor as:
$$\begin{align}
g_{ij}&=e_i\cdot e_j
\end{align}$$
And the products of these vectors as:
$$\begin{align}
\bra{x}\cdot\bra{y}&=g^{ij}x_iy_j\\
\ket{x}\cdot\ket{y}&=g_{ij}x^iy^j\\
\braket{x|y}&=x_iy^i\\
\ket{x}\bra{y}&=x^iy_je_ie^j
\end{align}$$
$$\begin{align}
\ket{x}\cdot\ket{y}&=x^iy^j e_i\times e_j\\
&=x^iy^j \epsilon_{ijk}e_k
\end{align}$$


$$\begin{align}
\vec{a}\times\vec{b}&=\epsilon_{ijk}e_ia^jb^k
\end{align}$$
