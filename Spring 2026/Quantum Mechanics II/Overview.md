### Course Overview
**Class:** Quantum Mechanics II (PHYS-6520)
**Instructor:** Damien West, Senior Lecturer (westd2@rpi.edu)

**Description:** Learning fundamental concepts and developing advanced problem solving skills in quantum mechanics. Quantum mechanics with Tensor Operators; Symmetry in Quantum Mechanics; Time-Independent Perturbation Theory; Time-Dependent Potentials and Time-Dependent Perturbation Theory; Scattering Theory: Scattering as a Time-Dependent Perturbation, Born Approximation, Partial Waves; Relativistic Quantum Mechanics.

**Learning Outcomes:** Upon successfully completing the course students will demonstrate an ability to apply concepts and theories of Quantum Mechanics in advanced problem-solving tasks, as well as the ability to make use of physical principles along with mathematics to describe quantum mechanical phenomena.

**Assessment & Grading:** Regular homework assignments (roughly every other week) are taken from the textbook and will be posted and submitted on the course LMS site. Late homework will not be accepted without prior approval from the instructor.
